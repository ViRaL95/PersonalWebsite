Arduino Metal Detector

A circuit that can sense when a metal object is close to it.

I made this project to test my soldering skills and my ability to be able to design the neatest circuit possible (very few wires and neat soldering). To the right you are able to see the schematic for the circuit I have built.
After gathering all materials I made the inductor using the 28 gauge magnetic wire by tying it around a wooden rod that I made. I then soldered the components onto a printed circuit board. The video of the final project can be seen to the right.
As you can see the circuit is soldered and fully functioning.
I thought this was a cool project to make because I was able to build my own inductor using magnetic wire, it also involved coding the Arduino Uno, and I was able to enhance my design skills by neatly soldering all components onto a printed circuit board. Below is a picture of the final product.
Parts List
Switch
Piezo Buzzer
28 Gauge Magnetic Wire
2 10K Ohm Resistors, 2 1.2K Ohm Resistors
2 100 NanoFarad Capacitors, 2 22 NanoFarad Capacitors
